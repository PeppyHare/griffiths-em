    So moving the origin (or, what amounts to the same thing, moving the charge) can radically alter a multipole expansion. The monopole moment does not change, since the total charge is obviously independent of the coordinate system. (In Fig. 3.32, the monopole term was unaffected when we moved q away from the origin - it's just that it was no longer the whole story: a dipole term - and for that matter all higher poles - appeared as well.)
